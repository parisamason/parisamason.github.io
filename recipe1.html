Corn Ribs

History of the Dish

Corn ribs are a modern twist on traditional grilled corn. The side/snack became popular media platforms like TikTok, where home cooks discovered that slicing corn into rib-like strips creates a fun, crispy snack. The technique is inspired by Mexican street corn and Southern-style grilled corn, combining bold spices and flavorful toppings.
Learn more at: Corn Ribs

Recipe

Corn ribs are a really tasty, crunchy way to enjoy corn on the cob. The corn is cut into curved strips, seasoned, and baked or air-fried until the edges curl like ribs. (When my aunt makes them she usually air-fries them.) They make the perfect snack, appetizer, or side dish.

Ingredients

  • 4 ears of corn
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Parmesan Cheese
  • Cilantro for looks and a limey taste (optional)
  • Parsley flakes for looks (optional)

Directions

  1. Carefully cut each ear of corn into quarters lengthwise.
  2. Place the corn strips in a large bowl.
  3. Mix olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, chili powder, salt, and pepper.
  4. Coat the corn evenly with the seasoning mixture.
  5. Air fry at 400°F for 12–14 minutes or bake at 425°F for 20–25 minutes.
  6. Serve with a little bit of lime juice, chopped cilantro, parsley flakes, and parmesan cheese.

Twists You Can Make

  • Mexican Elote Style: Toss with chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, and lime juice. Top with cotija cheese, cilantro, and a creamy avocado or sriracha mayo sauce.
  • Cajun or Creole: Use a Cajun seasoning blend or create your own with cayenne pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder.
  • Thai Inspired: Season with a Thai Pad Kra Pao mix or a combination of ginger, garlic, and red pepper flakes.
