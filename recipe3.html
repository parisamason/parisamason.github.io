Corn Ribs

Corn ribs are a really tasty, crunchy way to enjoy corn on the cob. The corn is cut into curved strips, seasoned, and baked or air-fried until the edges curl like ribs. (When my aunt makes them she usually air-fries them.) They make the perfect snack, appetizer, or side dish.

Here are the Ingredients, Recipe, Twists You Can Make, and Directions

Learn more

History of The Dish

Corn ribs are a modern twist on traditional grilled corn. The side/snack became popular media platforms like TikTok, where home cooks discovered that slicing corn into rib-like strips creates a fun, crispy snack. The technique is inspired by Mexican street corn and Southern-style grilled corn, combining bold spices and flavorful toppings.



Ingredients



  • 4 ears of corn
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder

  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Parmesan Cheese
  • Cilantro for looks and a limey taste (optional)
  • Parsley flakes for looks (optional)

    • Recipe and Directions

    • Carefully cut each ear of corn into quarters lengthwise.
    • Place the corn strips in a large bowl.
    • Mix olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, chili powder, salt, and pepper.
    • Coat the corn evenly with the seasoning mixture
    • Air fry at 400°F for 12–14 minutes or bake at 425°F for 20–25 minutes.
    • Serve with a little bit of lime juice, chopped cilantro, parsley flakes, and parmesan cheese.

    Twist You Can Make



    Elote Corn Ribs
